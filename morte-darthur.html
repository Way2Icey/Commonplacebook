King Arthur struggle with the news of news of lancelot taking his wife challengs his pride and forces him to be hesitant to confront the situation "For Sir Launcelot is an hardy knight, and all ye know he is the best knight among us all." Arthurs pride in his knight blinds his reality, Betraying someone he deeply values causes the hesitation in his mind. Lancelot's determination serves as an example of how pride and the pride his queen has for him, push him to not appear as cowardly. "Fair nephew, said Sir Launcelot, I marvel me much why ye say thus, sithen the queen hath sent for me" lancelots pride in his perceived abilities by others causes him to act against reason, showcasing the mirroring between both Arthur and Lancelot's prideful decision making.
