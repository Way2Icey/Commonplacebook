The Wife of Bath's Prologue and Tale

The Knights pride in his status allows him to act without regard for consequences. "He saw a maiden walking before him, Of which maiden straightway, despite all she could do,By utter force, he took away her maidenhead;" The knights lack of humility and respect demonstrates how unchecked pride allows violent behavior. The queen then humbles the knight with her given task, the polarity between the knight being so prideful in his position to act violently on a women, to then depend on finding answers from women to avoid execution. Symbolizing the need to relinquish pride and seek understanding of those affected. The knights pride is tested again by the Old womans wisdom, the women knows the knight is prideful and is reflected in her answer. "Sovereignty over their husbands" The Duality of experience that the knight faces being the abused and abuser showing the prideful consequences exist in both.
